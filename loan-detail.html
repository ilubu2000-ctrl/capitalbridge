📐 How the 50/40/10 Structure Works

50% from a conventional bank lender · 40% from SBA via a Certified Development Company (CDC) · 10% borrower down payment. Startups or special-purpose properties may require 15–20% down.

✅ Advantages
  • Lowest possible fixed interest rate
  • Only 10% down payment
  • 25-year fixed rate = predictable payments
  • Up to $5.5M SBA portion (total project can be larger)
  • Ideal for owner-occupied commercial real estate
⚠️ Considerations
  • Real estate or fixed assets only — no working capital
  • Must create or retain 1 job per $75K SBA funds
  • Business must occupy 51%+ of property
  • More complex two-lender structure
  • Longer closing timeline (60–120 days)

🎯 Best For

Commercial Real Estate Purchase Owner-Occupied Building Heavy Equipment Hotel / Motel Manufacturing Facility Medical Office

📍 Rural Area Definition

Business must be located in a city or town with a population under 50,000 that is not adjacent to a metropolitan area. Check eligibility at the USDA eligibility map.

✅ Advantages
  • Much higher loan limits than SBA ($25M)
  • More flexible ownership/citizenship requirements
  • 30-year terms for real estate
  • Supports investment properties (not just owner-occupied)
  • Great for hotels, self-storage, manufacturing in rural areas
⚠️ Considerations
  • Rural location required (<50K population)
  • Slower approval than SBA Express
  • Annual renewal fee on guaranteed portion
  • More limited lender pool

🎯 Best For

Rural Hotel / Motel Self Storage Facility Manufacturing Plant Investment Real Estate (Rural) Non-Citizen Owned Business Large Projects $5M+
